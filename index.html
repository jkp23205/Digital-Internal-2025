Biblical Figures

In the Bible, there are several influencial and significant people. Mary, mother of Jesus, Peter, John the Evangelist, and even God himself. However, these three figures not only had a direct impact of Jesus' life, but the shaping and delivery of the Bible's message itself. Who better to learn about, than Jesus' betrayer, the first witness to His ressurection, and the wisest king who had a thousand consorts.

Judas Iscariot

These days the name "Judas" is treated as a symbol of betrayal, disrespect or lies. Many are familiar with the Lady Gaga's song "Judas", which is a song with many references to the Bible. Others may know the character "Judas" in the walking dead, where the show makes more biblical references. Yet at the heart of it all, is a young man meant to follow Jesus' path, who instead ended up 30 pieces of silver richer, and less of a heart. Go on a journey and learn to stay loyal to Jesus and dive deeper into Judas' character.

Mary Magdalene

With up to six different "Marys" in the bible, it can be difficult to distinguish between each woman. Most main differentiators being their children or husbands. But women in the Bible are so much more than wives or mothers, carriers of life, and most importantly, followers of Jesus and fufillers of his plan. The first one to witness Jesus' ressurection was none other than Mary Magdalene, an outcast of society and known as one of the most loyal followers of Christ. If you want to know more about her story as an outcasted woman, and her witness to the ressurection, learn more about her story and role in the bible.

King Solomon

To outsiders, King Solomon is one of the wisest, wealthiest, and most powerful kings in Israel, and the Bible. Or, to some, he is seen as the symbol of greed due to his plentiful wealth, or lust from his thousand consorts. Although, he is known for all these things, he was one of the most loyal followers of God in the old testament, and one of the many influential writers of the Bible. From writing lines of poetic love in the Song of Songs, to Proverbs, one of the most well known books of the Bible. So if Biblical, poetic writing is more your thing, Solomon's writings may teach you a thing or two.
